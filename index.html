Production flow — EEW Erndtebrück
1
Raw material reception & inspection
Carbon steel coils/plates (SAWL 450, X65QS) and CRA strip (Alloy 625/825) from VAGB Linz received. MTRs verified vs. MPS. Dimensional checks, PMI, visual inspection per DNV-ST-F101.
VAGB sub-supply100% MTR verification
2
Plate forming & LSAW pipe fabrication
Steel plates progressively formed via JCO/UOE pressing into cylindrical shells. LSAW welding (SAW process) for longitudinal seam. Pre-heating applied for wall thicknesses >25 mm.
SAW — submerged arcWPS/PQR qualified
3
CRA internal weld overlay / cladding
Internal surface prepared by blasting to Sa 2.5. CRA applied via orbital GTAW/mechanised SAW overlay in multiple passes. Interpass temperature controlled <150°C. Bond line inspection per ASTM E1815 / ASTM A578.
Critical step100% UT bond check
4
Post-weld heat treatment (PWHT)
Solution annealing at 1040–1080°C (Alloy 625) or stress relief as applicable. Controlled heating/cooling rates per qualified procedure. Thermocouple monitoring with certified charts.
ITP hold point
5
Dimensional, mechanical & NDE testing
Full body UT, RT/digital RT on seam welds; MPI on pipe body. Hardness survey, Charpy impact at –20°C, tensile test per heat/lot. CRA overlay chemistry by XRF/OES. Hydrostatic test to 1.5× SMYS.
TPIA witnessQE LNG review
6
Final inspection & release
Final visual/dimensional, marking (heat no., material grade, schedule), FBE coating, end protectors. MRB (Manufacturer Record Book) compilation. Release per ITP hold/witness points with TPIA sign-off.
MRB complete
Applicable codes & standards
Type I
DNV-ST-F101 (2021-08) — SAWL 450 SFD C, CRA Clad: Alloy 625 (UNS N06625). Offshore pipeline code; highest structural & CRA requirements.
Type II
DNV-ST-F101 (2021-08) — SAWL 450 SFD C, CRA Clad: Alloy 825 (LC2242 / UNS N08825). Cost-optimised alloy for lower-severity zones.
Type III
API 5L (46th ed.) — X65QS PSL 2, UNS N06625 cladding. For riser / high-pressure sections with sour service duty (NACE MR0175).

Recommendations for future projects
01
Early CRA alloy procurement locking
Secure Alloy 625 and 825 strip orders minimum 6 months ahead of pipe production start to mitigate mill capacity constraints at specialist suppliers such as VAGB.
02
Digital ITP management platform
Implement a web-based ITP tracking system shared across all parties to replace email chains and spreadsheet-based hold-point management.
03
PAUT as primary NDE method
Adopt phased-array UT as the default NDE tool for seam welds and CRA bond inspection, reducing RT film cost and improving throughput.
04
Electric induction PWHT
Transition PWHT furnaces to electric induction heating powered by renewable energy to reduce carbon footprint and improve temperature uniformity.
05
HIB qualification pre-programme
For future projects including HIBs, conduct bending and heat treatment qualification trials at least 4 months prior to production start.
